The Foundation

What is General Skilled Migration?

General Skilled Migration (GSM) is the umbrella for Australia’s permanent and provisional visas awarded to skilled workers on the basis of merit — not an employer or family sponsor. Selection runs through a single, transparent points-tested system administered via SkillSelect.

You lodge an Expression of Interest (EOI), score points against fixed criteria, and the Department of Home Affairs issues invitations to the highest-ranking candidates in periodic invitation rounds. A positive skills assessment for your nominated occupation is the gateway to the entire program.

Truvia’s role is strategic: we identify the subclass and state that best fit your profile, then engineer your claim to extract every legitimate point available.

Subclass 491

Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) Visa

A 5-year provisional visa for skilled migrants willing to live and work in regional Australia. It carries the largest points bonus in the program and leads to permanent residence via the Subclass 191 after three years of regional residence and income.

The 491 runs in two distinct streams — choose the one that matches who is supporting your application.

Stream 01

Regional Sponsored Stream

Nominated by a participating state or territory government to address skill shortages in designated regional areas. The most common route into the 491.

+15 points for regional nomination

Family Sponsored Stream

Frequently Asked

GSM questions, answered

How many points do I need for GSM?+
You need a minimum of 65 points to submit a valid EOI, but the invitation cut-off is often considerably higher and varies by occupation. Many in-demand occupations see invitations issued at 85–95+ points, so the goal is always to maximise rather than just meet the minimum.
Do I need a skills assessment before applying?+
Yes. A positive skills assessment from the relevant assessing authority for your nominated occupation is mandatory for every GSM subclass and must be valid at the time of invitation. We confirm the correct authority and prepare your evidence before you lodge an EOI.
What is the difference between 189 and 491?+
The 189 is an immediate permanent visa with no sponsorship and full freedom of location. The 491 is a 5-year provisional regional visa that gives +15 points and transitions to permanent residence (Subclass 191) after three years of regional residence and income.
Can my partner and children be included?+
Yes. Your partner and dependent children can be included in a single GSM application. A skilled partner may also add points to your claim, and concessions apply if your partner is an Australian citizen/PR or you are applying as a single applicant.
How long does the process take?+
Timelines vary by subclass and occupation, but most applicants should plan for 6–12 months from invitation to grant, plus preparation time for skills assessment, English testing, and EOI before that. Regional 491 applications are often invited faster.
