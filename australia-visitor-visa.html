Overview

What the Subclass 600 covers

The Subclass 600 Visitor visa allows you to travel to Australia for a temporary stay — to holiday, see family, or take part in approved business visitor activities. Most grants allow a stay of up to 3, 6 or 12 months per entry, depending on your circumstances.

Decisions hinge on the Genuine Temporary Entrant principle: you must satisfy the department that you intend a genuine, temporary visit and will depart before your visa expires. A clear purpose, sound finances and ties to your home country are central.

Frequently Asked

Visitor visa questions

How long can I stay on a Subclass 600?+
Most visitor visas allow a stay of 3, 6 or 12 months per entry, set by the case officer based on your circumstances and purpose. The grant notice specifies your permitted stay and whether single or multiple entries apply.
Can I work on a visitor visa?+
No. The Subclass 600 does not permit work in Australia. You may undertake limited study of up to three months and approved business visitor activities such as meetings or conferences — but not paid local employment.
What is the Genuine Temporary Entrant requirement?+
It is the principle that you genuinely intend to visit temporarily and will leave before your visa expires. Strong financial evidence, a clear purpose and solid ties to your home country all support a genuine temporary intent.
Does travel history matter?+
Yes. A record of complying with visa conditions on previous trips strengthens your application. If you have limited travel history, a well-evidenced, consistent application becomes even more important — which is where our guidance helps.
